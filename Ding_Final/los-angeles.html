01 Griffith Observatory

Griffith Observatory is a famous landmark in Los Angeles, California, located on the southern slope of Mount Hollywood in Griffith Park. The observatory features public telescopes, space and science exhibits, and a planetarium that offers fascinating shows about the universe. From its terraces, visitors can enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of Los Angeles and the Hollywood Sign. The observatory plays an important role in promoting astronomy education and public science awareness. It has also been featured in many movies and TV shows, such as La La Land and Rebel Without a Cause. Whether you come for the science or the stunning city views, Griffith Observatory is a must-visit destination in Los Angeles.

02 Getty Museum

The Getty Museum, officially known as the J. Paul Getty Museum, is one of the most famous art museums in Los Angeles, California. It was founded by oil magnate J. Paul Getty and opened to the public in 1974. The museum has two locations: the Getty Center in Brentwood and the Getty Villa in Pacific Palisades. The Getty Center is known for its modern architecture, beautiful gardens, and stunning views of Los Angeles, while the Getty Villa focuses on ancient Greek, Roman, and Etruscan art. The museum’s collections include European paintings, sculptures, decorative arts, photographs, and manuscripts. Admission is free, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

03 Santa Monica Beach

Santa Monica Beach is one of the most famous beaches in California, located just west of downtown Los Angeles. It’s known for its wide sandy shore, the iconic Santa Monica Pier with its Ferris wheel, and beautiful ocean views. Visitors can enjoy swimming, biking, or simply relaxing in the sun while watching the Pacific waves. At sunset, the beach offers stunning views that attract photographers and travelers from around the world.

04 Food

In Los Angeles, I tried a variety of delicious foods from different restaurants. From street snacks to fine dining, every meal was a new and exciting experience. I sampled savory street tacos bursting with flavor, sweet and creamy desserts from local bakeries, and indulgent dishes at high-end restaurants on the Strip. Each place offered something unique, from exotic international cuisines to modern takes on classic American favorites. The vibrant food scene in Las Vegas not only satisfied my hunger but also introduced me to creative culinary combinations and flavors I had never tried before, making every meal a memorable adventure.
