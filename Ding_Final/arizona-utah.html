01 Forrest Gump Point

Forrest Gump Point is a famous viewpoint on U.S. Highway 163 near Monument Valley, Utah. It became well-known after appearing in the movie Forrest Gump (1994). The spot marks where Forrest decided to stop running and go home. Visitors can see a long, straight road surrounded by stunning red rock formations. Many people stop to take photos and enjoy the peaceful desert scenery. It’s a must-visit destination for movie lovers and travelers alike. The location captures the beauty and spirit of the American Southwest. Watching the sunset here makes the landscape even more magical and unforgettable.

02 Horseshoe Bend

Horseshoe Bend is a breathtaking natural landmark located near Page, Arizona. It’s a dramatic curve of the Colorado River that forms a perfect horseshoe shape, surrounded by steep red cliffs. The overlook sits about 1,000 feet above the river, offering one of the most stunning views in the American Southwest. Visitors can hike a short trail from the parking area to reach the viewpoint. It’s especially beautiful at sunrise and sunset when the light changes the color of the canyon walls. Horseshoe Bend is a popular spot for photographers, nature lovers, and anyone who enjoys unforgettable landscapes. The view truly shows the power and beauty of nature.

03 Hoover Dam

Hoover Dam is a massive concrete arch-gravity dam located on the Colorado River, on the border between Arizona and Nevada. It was completed in 1936 during the Great Depression and was considered one of the greatest engineering achievements of its time. The dam was built to provide flood control, water storage, and hydroelectric power for the growing Southwest region. Its reservoir, Lake Mead, is one of the largest man-made lakes in the United States and a popular spot for boating and recreation. Hoover Dam generates electricity for millions of people in Nevada, Arizona, and California. Visitors can explore the visitor center, take guided tours, and enjoy panoramic views from observation decks.
